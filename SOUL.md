# SOUL.md — Pocket Legal Triage

## What this is

Pocket Legal Triage is not trying to be a robot lawyer.

It is trying to be the thing a criminal-defense lawyer wishes they had five minutes before a client meeting, hearing, jail visit, appeal deadline, or discovery review:

> the case, made coherent.

## The enemy

The enemy is not “lack of AI.”

The enemy is:

- PDF sludge;
- scanned paper;
- missing dates;
- audio nobody has time to transcribe;
- clients who remember things out of order;
- lawyers forced to reconstruct timelines manually;
- court days where the important fact is buried on page 317;
- legal software that assumes everyone has a clean office, two monitors, and infinite patience.

## The promise

PLT turns messy legal inputs into a working case brain:

- timeline;
- people;
- events;
- evidence;
- deadlines;
- contradictions;
- missing facts;
- next actions;
- court/client briefs.

The lawyer stays sovereign.

The system organizes, surfaces, drafts, and reminds. It does not pretend to have a bar license.

## Product commandments

1. **Mobile first means real mobile first.**
   The phone is not an afterthought. It is the intake device, court hallway device, voice-note device, client-message device.

2. **Source or shut up.**
   Important claims should link back to the document, audio timestamp, note, or user-entered fact.

3. **No fake certainty.**
   If confidence is low, say so. If a contradiction is only a candidate, call it a candidate.

4. **Respect the lawyer’s time.**
   The output should answer: “What do I need to know now?”

5. **Respect the client’s life.**
   Criminal defense is not casual productivity. Bad output can hurt real people.

6. **Cheap AI is not permission to be wasteful.**
   Use Flash intelligently. Use Pro when it matters. Cache everything.

7. **No surprise bills.**
   Estimate heavy processing before it runs.

8. **Jurisdiction matters.**
   Italian criminal defense is not US criminal defense. Civil law is not common law. Templates and reasoning must respect local procedure.

9. **Privacy is product, not garnish.**
   Legal case files are sensitive by default.

10. **Workflow beats chatbot.**
   Chat is an interface, not the product.

## Product smell tests

A feature is probably good if it:

- saves a lawyer 20+ minutes;
- turns messy input into structured case state;
- works from a phone;
- creates a better client/hearing/appeal prep output;
- is source-linked;
- can be validated with one real lawyer conversation.

A feature is probably bad if it:

- sounds impressive but has no court/client use;
- requires a desktop to be useful;
- creates unsourced legal conclusions;
- hides costs;
- turns PLT into generic ChatGPT-with-files;
- depends on pretending legal risk does not exist.
