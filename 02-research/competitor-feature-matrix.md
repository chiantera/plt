# Competitor Feature Matrix

## Opus 2

Category: litigation platform / matter context / AI workbench.

Emphasis:

- context-enabled AI;
- litigation workflows;
- document summaries;
- case-wide insights;
- prompt library;
- chronology building;
- secure/control/trust principles.

PLT response:

- keep chronology/timeline as core;
- beat them on mobile-first criminal-defense intake;
- avoid enterprise litigation platform sprawl.

## Relativity aiR

Category: enterprise eDiscovery / litigation / investigations.

Emphasis:

- fit-for-purpose legal AI;
- defensible results;
- detailed explanations;
- review, privilege, case strategy;
- Azure OpenAI/security/compliance;
- agentic workflows.

PLT response:

- do not compete on million-document enterprise review;
- borrow trust/defensibility language;
- focus on small criminal-defense matter prep.

## Everlaw AI Deep Dive

Category: eDiscovery / large corpus Q&A / evidence discovery.

Emphasis:

- search millions of docs;
- zero query limits;
- direct citations;
- insufficient-info behavior;
- unified workflows to Storybuilder/Writing Assistant.

PLT response:

- citation/source-linking is mandatory;
- “not enough info” behavior required;
- use smaller-scale case corpus and mobile UX as wedge.

## Vincent by Clio

Category: mobile legal AI assistant.

Emphasis:

- Authority Anywhere;
- iPhone legal assistant;
- voice-to-insight;
- legal research on the go;
- analyze complaints/briefs/exhibits;
- direct links to authorities;
- cross-platform sync;
- Clio ecosystem/security.

PLT response:

- strongest direct validation of mobile-first;
- must differentiate from general legal research;
- focus on criminal-defense case file triage, OCR/audio/discovery, source links to case materials.

## Claude for Legal

Category: foundation model platform entering legal vertical.

Emphasis:

- legal plugins;
- integrations with legal tools;
- practice-area customization;
- legal playbooks;
- onboarding interview;
- document comprehension;
- human in loop;
- broad ecosystem.

PLT response:

- thin wrappers are unsafe;
- build workflow/data model/product surface;
- DeepSeek-first can differentiate cost and Italian/civil-law quality;
- include practice/matter onboarding.

## PLT wedge

PLT should be:

> mobile-first criminal-defense case triage for small/solo lawyers.

Not:

- enterprise eDiscovery;
- general legal research;
- broad legal AI assistant;
- Claude wrapper;
- Clio clone.

## Feature priorities for alpha based on competitor scan

1. Mobile-first upload/capture.
2. Source-linked timeline.
3. Missing info/questions.
4. Brief generation.
5. DeepSeek Flash/Pro routing.
6. Cost/usage transparency.
7. Demo sample case.
